### Post 1 — Small Tweaks. Big Peaks.

Small tweaks. Big peaks.

One of the biggest mistakes creators make is assuming growth only comes from massive changes. In reality, some of the biggest wins come from small, strategic adjustments.

Recently, I suggested one simple tweak to a channel's publishing strategy: move the V2 Channel upload to Friday.

That one change performed way better than expected.

The video took off Friday night, carried through the weekend, and even hit trending.

The ripple effect was strong:
- 216% more views than usual in the last 28 days
- 140% more views from YouTube recommendations

Before you overhaul everything, ask yourself:
What is one small tweak that could create a big peak?

Often, the next level is not a reinvention.
It is a refinement.

### Post 1 — Open Loops vs. Closed Loops: The AI Strategy That Actually Compounds
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/ericosiu_there-are-3-levels-to-how-teams-are-using-activity-7471958389653274624-z1ZE/

The typical AI strategy right now is querying an LLM.

You ask Claude something, get a good answer, paste it somewhere, close the tab. Tomorrow you open a fresh chat and do it again. That's an open loop.

You're the wire connecting every step, so the second you stop, the whole thing stops. Nothing it learned today shows up tomorrow.

This is why I think everyone should be working in closed loops.

A closed loop doesn't need you in the middle. It takes an input, does the work, looks at what came back, and feeds that result into its own next run. The output becomes the next input.

That's the whole trick, and it's the part that changes everything, because now the system is improving on its own instead of waiting for you to prompt it again.

Here's what that looks like for us with paid creative:

We feed in the customer pain, the proof, the offer, the channel data. Agents spin up a hundred ad variants off that. We launch, the performance data comes back, and instead of me reading it and starting a new brief, the loop reads it. It scales the winners, kills the losers, saves what worked, and runs again — now built on everything the last cycle figured out. Nobody resets to zero.

That's the difference in one line. An open loop forgets. A closed loop compounds.

And the compounding is the part people sleep on. The value was never the hundred variants. It's that the loop is a little sharper every cycle and you're not in the chair for most of them.

Compare it to selling labor. One person nails the creative, the next is fine, the third is learning on your client's dime. The output swings with whoever's working that week. A closed loop doesn't swing, and that predictability is what clients actually pay for.

"Robot stuff for robots, human stuff for humans." Most teams never draw the line, so their best people stay stuck cranking variants by hand and the loop never gets built.

Claude Code and Codex can build this for you today.

The only question is whether you're building a loop that compounds, or just saving a few prompts and calling it a strategy.
