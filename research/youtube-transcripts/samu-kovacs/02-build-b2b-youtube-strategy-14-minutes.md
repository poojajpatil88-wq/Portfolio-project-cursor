Thumbnails are one of the biggest levers you can pull on YouTube. You can make the world's most useful video but if the thumbnail isn't properly designed, no one will click.

How viewers interact:
1. Thumbnail catches their eye (first thing they see while scrolling)
2. They read the title if the thumbnail caught attention
3. They look back at the thumbnail to confirm both match
4. If thumbnail + title give the impression the video is worth their time, they click

**Current thumbnails problem:** Too much text, lack of clarity, nothing that catches the eye, tells rather than shows.

**The fix:** Make the thumbnail simple enough to catch eyes at first glance. Match it with the title — not by copying it, but by complementing it so the viewer can make sense of the video and decide if they're interested.
